Main subject of this video:
1. The Indian Railways: Origin and Transition of Control The Indian Railways originated under British rule, but control was eventually transferred to the Indian government. Since then, the government has established and implemented the working policies that govern the rail system today.
2. Changes and Improvements Since Independence (1947) Since gaining freedom in 1947, we have seen various improvements in the railway system. However, the question remains: has this progress been distributed equally across all classes and categories of travel?
3. Regulations in Reserved vs. General Coaches In 1st, 2nd, and 3rd AC, as well as Sleeper classes, the booking system is strictly regulated; not a single additional ticket is allowed once seats are full. Conversely, for General tickets, there seem to be no regulations regarding price collection or the volume of ticket sales.
4. Addressing Technical Issues and Booking Policies When problems or "bugs" arise in the booking system, the Railway Department attempts to implement solutions. For example, in 2025, they deleted 52 lakh accounts that lacked Aadhaar authentication and reduced the advance booking window from four months to two months. I am seeking more specific details regarding these actions.
5. The Divide Between Public Servants and the Common Man Active government bodies, public servants, high-ranking police officers, lawyers, advocates, the PM, CMs, MLAs, and MPs are all capable of purchasing higher-priced tickets or finding alternative ways to journey. However, what about the common man? His daily wages are often only around ₹1,000 to ₹2,000 maximum, leaving him with no other options.
